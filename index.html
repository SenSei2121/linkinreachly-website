Flagship Work

Semper Signum & Xvary

Founder · 2026 · sempersignum.com (institutional) · xvary.com (consumer)

Problem
Equity research follows repeatable patterns — reading filings, cross-referencing data, writing memos — but analysts still spend days on each report. The same core problem applies at different scales: institutional (hedge funds, RIAs) and consumer (individual investors).
Built
Agentic system that ingests data, runs multi-step analysis, and generates structured research reports. Designed the orchestration layer to catch step-level failures before they corrupt downstream output. Shipped two products from the same core: Semper Signum for institutions, Xvary for consumers — different interfaces, different trust requirements, same underlying system.
Result
End-to-end pipeline: raw data in, formatted equity research report out. Both products live and validating with target users.
Insight
Full automation breaks when data sufficiency varies across cases. Human-in-the-loop is not a compromise — it is a design requirement when inputs are unpredictable. Building for two audiences from the same core forced clarity on what is platform and what is surface.
Agentic Orchestration Multi-step Pipelines Data Ingestion LLMs Consumer + Institutional

Sentari AI

Founder · 2025–2026 · withsentari.com · App Store + Web

Problem
Fitness devices track your body passively. Nothing tracks how you think. Journaling exists, but no system learns from it or adapts to you.
Built
Voice and text journaling with memory, personality modeling, thematic tagging, and adaptive feedback. Screened 600+ applicants, hired and managed a 40-person cross-functional team. Shipped to App Store and web.
Result
1,900 users. Ran SEO, outreach, and content experiments to drive acquisition. Growth plateaued — journaling demands active input every session, unlike passive trackers. Made the call to wind down rather than force a market that was not there.
Insight
Building the product was not the hard part. Distribution was. Products that require behavior change fail at habit formation, not model quality. I came out of this knowing exactly where my growth gap is.
Whisper GPT Memory Systems Semantic Tagging Voice AI

Cerno Labs

Founder · 2024 · AI Search for Enterprise

Problem
Enterprise users searching multilingual, high-context domains (Egypt, Japan) get results they cannot verify or trust.
Built
RAG pipeline with grounding verification, caching, and agentic query layer — memory, domain filters, source inspection. Pitched to telecom executives. Led VC fundraising in New York and Tokyo.
Result
Technically sound product, but enterprise users kept saying the same thing: they did not want better search — they wanted answers they could trust. That reframed the entire product question.
Insight
Explainability builds trust. Novelty does not. Users choose a system they can verify over one they cannot.
RAG Grounding Verification Caching Agentic Query Multilingual

Thinking

AI Systems

  • Multi-step pipelines fail because input quality compounds at every step.
  • Hallucination is structural, not incidental. Design for when it occurs.
  • Human-in-the-loop is not a fallback. It is an architecture decision.
  • The hardest part of automation is knowing where to stop. The value is in drawing the line correctly.

Product

  • Behavior change is harder than feature quality. Passive products scale; active ones struggle.
  • Explainability builds trust. Novelty does not.
  • Overengineering is the most common founder trap in AI.

GTM

  • Distribution is system design, not a sales function. I learned this the hard way.
  • If anyone can clone it in a weekend, your moat is not technology.
  • Early traction does not equal repeatable commercial success. That gap is real.
